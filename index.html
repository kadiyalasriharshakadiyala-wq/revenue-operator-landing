Operator Mindset

How I think about
pipeline and revenue.

The mechanics are learnable. The judgment — knowing what to prioritize, who to target, what to say — is what separates reps from operators.

ICP Logic

I don't chase accounts. I score them.

Before I send a single email, I ask: does this account have the budget trigger, the urgency signal, and the decision authority structure to close in a reasonable cycle? If the answer is no on any two — skip it. ICP discipline is what keeps conversion rates sane when volume scales up.

My ICP filter (B2B SaaS, SMB/MM)
→ 10–200 employees
→ Active hiring in ops/sales
→ Funded or revenue-positive
→ Recent trigger event
Outbound Philosophy

Relevance > Volume. Always.

I've seen what "100 emails/day" looks like when they're not relevant — burned domains, burned reputation, zero pipeline. My outbound is built around one question: "Why should this specific person care, right now?" The answer determines the hook, the sequence, and the channel mix.

Messaging

I lead with the cost of the problem, not the product.

Nobody replies to "we help companies like yours improve X by Y%." They reply when you name their exact problem, frame the cost of inaction, and make the ask feel like a no-brainer. Every message I write is built on: problem → cost → pivot → ask.

Sales Execution

I treat every deal like a project, not a conversation.

Mutual action plans from the first call. Champion mapping before I run the demo. I know who signs, who blocks, and who influences — before I ask for the close. Deals don't ghost me; I see it coming three steps out and re-engage before it happens.

Most outbound fails before
the first email is sent.

Not because of messaging. Because of targeting. Here's exactly how I approach it — judge the thinking.

1

Start with ICP discipline

If the account doesn't have budget, urgency, or a clear decision structure — it's not worth touching. Volume doesn't fix bad targeting.

2

Build around trigger signals

Hiring, funding, team expansion — these create timing. Outbound without timing is noise.

3

Message around cost, not features

People don't respond to product value. They respond to problems that are already costing them.

4

Run deals like projects

Multi-thread early. Map stakeholders. Drive next steps. I don't "hope deals close" — I control the process.
